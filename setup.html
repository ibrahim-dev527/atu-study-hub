Create Supabase Account

Free forever. This is where all student accounts and past question data live.

🌐 Create account + project
  1. 1
    Go to supabase.com and click "Start your project"
  2. 2
    Click "Sign Up" and sign up with GitHub (free) or emailIf you don't have GitHub, go to github.com first and create a free account — it takes 2 minutes.
  3. 3
    Once logged in, click "New Project"
  4. 4
    Fill the form:Project name: atu-study-hub
    Database Password: Create a strong password and WRITE IT DOWN
    Region: West EU (Ireland) — closest to Ghana
  5. 5
    Click "Create new project" and wait 1–2 minutes for it to set up
🔑 Copy your API Keys (VERY IMPORTANT)
  1. 1
    In Supabase, look at the left sidebar and click the ⚙️ gear icon at the very bottom
  2. 2
    Click "API" in the settings menu that opens
  3. 3
    Copy BOTH these values and paste them into a Notepad or Word document:📋 Project URL — looks like: https://abcdefgh.supabase.co
    📋 anon / public key — a very long code starting with eyJ...
🚨
DO NOT copy the "service_role" key! Only copy the anon / public key. The service_role key is dangerous and must never be put in a website file.

📋 How to edit each file (step by step)
  1. 1
    Right-click on login.html → "Open with" → choose Notepad (Windows) or TextEdit (Mac)Even better: download VS Code free at code.visualstudio.com — it's much easier
  2. 2
    Press Ctrl+F (Windows) or Cmd+F (Mac) to search
  3. 3
    Search for: YOUR_SUPABASE_URL
  4. 4
    Find these two lines near the bottom of the file and change them:
BEFORE (what's already in the file):
const SUPABASE_URL = 'YOUR_SUPABASE_URL'; const SUPABASE_ANON_KEY = 'YOUR_SUPABASE_ANON_KEY';
AFTER (replace with your real values — keep the quotes!):
const SUPABASE_URL = 'https://abcxyz.supabase.co'; // ← your actual URL const SUPABASE_ANON_KEY = 'eyJhbGciOiJIUzI1Ni...'; // ← your actual key
  1. 5
    Press Ctrl+S to save
  2. 6
    Repeat steps 1–5 for: signup.html, dashboard.html, user-dashboard.html, and past-questions.html
✅
That's the only code editing you'll ever do! Everything else after this is done through the Supabase website and your admin dashboard — no code.

Create File Storage Bucket

This is the online folder where PDF files are stored when you upload them.

🗂️ Create the past-questions bucket
  1. 1
    In Supabase, click "Storage" in the left sidebar
  2. 2
    Click "New bucket"
  3. 3
    Type the name: past-questions⚠️ Must use a hyphen ( - ) not a space. Must be exactly this spelling.
  4. 4
    Turn ON the toggle called "Public bucket"
  5. 5
    Click "Create bucket"
  6. 6
    Click on your new bucket → click "Policies" tab → click "New Policy"
  7. 7
    Select "For full customization"Policy name: allow_all | Check: ✅ SELECT ✅ INSERT | Target roles: anon, authenticated
  8. 8
    Click "Review" → "Save policy" ✅

Upload Past Questions

How to add PDFs so they appear on the website. No coding required.

🎉
This is how Bafiku uploads past questions day-to-day! Once the website is live, just open the Admin Dashboard, fill in a simple form, and click upload. The PDF appears on the website instantly.
📤 How to upload through the Admin Dashboard
  1. 1
    Open your browser and go to: yourwebsite.netlify.app/dashboard.html
  2. 2
    Log in with your admin email and password
  3. 3
    On the left side you'll see the Upload Past Question form. Fill it in:📝 Title: e.g. "Engineering Mathematics I – Final Exam"
    🏛️ Faculty: Select from dropdown
    🏫 Department: e.g. Civil Engineering (optional)
    📂 Level: Level 100, HND 1, etc.
    📅 Year: 2023, 2024, etc.
  4. 4
    Click the dashed box to choose your PDF from your computer (or drag & drop it)
  5. 5
    Click "⬆️ Upload Past Question" and watch the progress bar
  6. 6
    When you see ✅ success — the past question is LIVE on the website immediately!
  7. 7
    Go to past-questions.html → click "🆕 Recently Added" tab to see it appear at the top
💡
Why does "Recently Added" work now? The past-questions page now connects directly to Supabase. When you upload a PDF through the dashboard, it saves to the past_questions table with a timestamp. The "Recently Added" tab filters for questions uploaded in the last 7 days, sorted newest first.

Put Your Website Online

Free hosting with Netlify. Your site gets a public URL students can visit.

  1. 1
    Go to netlify.com and create a free account
  2. 2

Solving the Large PDF Problem

PDFs over 5MB are slow to upload and download. Here's exactly what to do.

⚠️
Why large PDFs are a problem: Students on mobile data in Ghana often have slow connections. A 15MB PDF could take 2+ minutes to download. Compressing it to 1MB makes it download in seconds. Also, Supabase's free plan has a 1GB storage limit — compressed PDFs let you store 10x more.
🗜️

Compress It (Best Option)

Go to ilovepdf.com/compress_pdf — free, no sign-up. Upload your PDF, compress it, download. A 15MB PDF often becomes 1–2MB with no visible quality loss.

📐

Target Size

Aim for under 3MB per past question file. Under 1MB is ideal. Most scanned exam papers should compress to under 2MB.

🔗

Use Google Drive Link

For very large files, upload to Google Drive, make it public, and paste the download link into the file_url field in the Supabase past_questions table directly.

💡
Google Drive download link format:
Get the file ID from the share link: drive.google.com/file/d/FILE_ID/view
Then use this as your download URL: https://drive.google.com/uc?export=download&id=FILE_ID
