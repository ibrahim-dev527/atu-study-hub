Create Your Supabase Account

Supabase is a free online database where student accounts and past question data are stored.

💡
What is Supabase?Think of Supabase like a very powerful Google Sheet that your website can read and write to automatically. When a student signs up, their info goes into Supabase. When you upload a past question, the file goes into Supabase. It is completely FREE for small projects.
🌐 Create your free Supabase account
  • 1
    Open your browser and go to: https://supabase.com
  • 2
    Click the big green button that says "Start your project"
  • 3
    Click "Sign up" and choose how to register:Recommended: Click "Continue with GitHub" — it's faster. If you don't have GitHub, go to github.com and create a free account first (takes 2 minutes).
  • 4
    Follow the prompts until you reach the Supabase dashboard (main page)
🚀 Create your Supabase project
  • 1
    On the Supabase dashboard, click the "New Project" button
  • 2
    Fill in the form:Project name: Type atu-study-hub
    Database Password: Create a strong password. WRITE IT DOWN — you'll need it later!
    Region: Choose "West EU (Ireland)" — it's the closest to Ghana
  • 3
    Click "Create new project"
  • 4
    ⏳ Wait about 2 minutes while Supabase sets up your project. You'll see a spinning animation.Don't close the page. Just wait. It will say "Project is ready" when done.
🔑 Find and copy your API keys
🚨
This step is very important! You need two special codes (called API keys) that let your website talk to Supabase. Copy them carefully and keep them safe.
  • 1
    In your Supabase project, look at the left sidebar
  • 2
    At the very bottom of the left sidebar, click the ⚙️ gear icon (Settings)
  • 3
    In the Settings menu that opens, click "API"
  • 4
    You will see two values. Copy BOTH of them and paste them into a Notepad or Word document:Value 1 — Project URL: Looks like https://abcdefgh.supabase.co
    Value 2 — anon / public key: A very long code starting with eyJ...
⚠️
Do NOT copy the "service_role" key! Only copy the "anon / public" key. The service_role key is dangerous to put in a website — never share it with anyone.
What to copyWhat it looks likeWhere it's labelled
Project URLhttps://xyz.supabase.coUnder "Project URL"
Anon KeyeyJhbGciOi... (very long)Under "anon public"

Connect Supabase to Your Website

Paste your API keys into 3 HTML files. This is the only time you'll touch the code.

📂
Which files to edit: You need to edit 3 files: login.html, signup.html, and dashboard.html. Do the same thing in all 3.
📝 How to open and edit each file
  • 1
    Right-click on login.html inside your atu-study-hub folderOn Windows: right-click → "Open with" → Notepad
    On Mac: right-click → "Open With" → TextEdit
    Best option: Use VS Code (free download at code.visualstudio.com)
  • 2
    The file will open showing a lot of code. Don't panic — you're only changing 2 small lines!
  • 3
    Press Ctrl+F (Windows) or Cmd+F (Mac) to open the Find tool
  • 4
    Type YOUR_SUPABASE_URL in the search box and press EnterIt will jump directly to that line in the code
  • 5
    Find the two lines that look like this:
Find this in the file (near the bottom, in the <script> section):
const SUPABASE_URL = 'YOUR_SUPABASE_URL'; const SUPABASE_ANON_KEY = 'YOUR_SUPABASE_ANON_KEY';
  • 6
    Replace YOUR_SUPABASE_URL with your actual URL (keep the single quotes!):Example result:
Change it to look like this (use YOUR actual values):
const SUPABASE_URL = 'https://abcdefghijkl.supabase.co'; const SUPABASE_ANON_KEY = 'eyJhbGciOiJIUzI1NiIsInR5cCI6IkpXVCJ9.eyJpc3...'; // ↑ These are YOUR actual values from Supabase Settings → API
  • 7
    Press Ctrl+S (Windows) or Cmd+S (Mac) to save the file
  • 8
    Repeat steps 1–7 for: signup.html and dashboard.htmlThe same 2 lines appear in all 3 files. Change them in each file.
✅
You're done with the code! That's the only time you need to touch the code files. Everything else is done through the Supabase website and your dashboard.

5

Create File Storage Bucket

This is the online folder where all your PDF past questions will be stored.

🗂️ Create the "past-questions" bucket
  • 1
    In Supabase, click "Storage" in the left sidebar (it looks like a database icon)
  • 2
    Click the "New bucket" button
  • 3
    In the Bucket name field, type exactly: past-questions⚠️ Use a hyphen ( - ) between the words, NOT a space. Spelling must be exact!
  • 4
    Find the toggle that says "Public bucket" and turn it ONThis allows students to download PDFs without needing to log in
  • 5
    Click "Create bucket"
  • 6
    Your bucket is created! Now set permissions. Click on "Policies" tab inside the bucket
  • 7
    Click "New Policy" → Select "For full customization"
  • 8
    Set:
    • Policy name: allow_all
    • Check boxes: ✅ SELECT ✅ INSERT
    • Target roles: select anon and authenticated
  • 9
    Click "Review" → "Save policy"
✅
Storage is ready! When you upload a PDF through the dashboard, it will be saved here automatically and a download link will be created.
6

Upload Past Questions (No Coding!)

This is the most important step for Bafiku. Two easy methods — pick whichever you prefer.

⭐ METHOD 1: Upload through the Dashboard (Easiest)
🎉
This is what Bafiku uses day-to-day! Once the website is live, just open the dashboard page, fill in a form, and click upload. No coding at all.
  • 1
    Open your browser and go to your website's dashboard page:Example: https://atu-study-hub.netlify.app/dashboard.html
    Or locally: Open dashboard.html in your browser
  • 2
    Log in with your email and password (the admin account you created via signup.html)
  • 3
    On the left side of the screen, you'll see the "Upload Past Question" form
  • 4
    Fill in the form fields:
    📝 Course/Paper Title: Type the full name, e.g. "Engineering Mathematics I – Final Exam"
    🏛️ Faculty: Select from dropdown (e.g. "Engineering")
    🏫 Department: Type department name (e.g. "Civil Engineering") — optional
    📂 Level: Select from dropdown (e.g. "Level 100")
    📅 Year: Select the exam year (e.g. "2023")
  • 5
    Click the upload box (the dashed rectangle that says "Click to choose PDF")A file browser opens — find your PDF on your computer and select it
    OR drag and drop the PDF directly onto the dashed box
  • 6
    Click the red "Upload Past Question" button
  • 7
    Watch the progress bar fill up — this shows the upload happeningDepending on internet speed, a 5MB PDF takes about 5–15 seconds
  • 8
    You'll see a green ✅ success message — the past question is now LIVE on your website!
  • 9
    To upload another one, just fill the form again and repeatYou can upload as many as you want, one by one
📁 METHOD 2: Upload directly through Supabase (Alternative)

Use this if the dashboard isn't working or you want to do it manually.

  • A
    Upload the PDF file first:Go to Supabase → Storage → past-questions bucket → Click "Upload files" → Select your PDF → Wait for upload to finish → Click on the uploaded file → Click "Get URL" or "Copy URL" → Save that URL
  • B
    Then add the record to the database:Go to Supabase → Table Editor → Click "past_questions" table → Click "Insert row" → Fill in: title, faculty, department, level, year, file_url (paste the URL from step A) → Click "Save"
💡
Method 1 does BOTH of these steps automatically with one button click. Method 2 is just if you need to upload without using your website.

Put Your Website Online

Deploy to Netlify — free hosting so anyone can visit your website from anywhere.

🚀 Deploy to Netlify (Free, No Credit Card)
  • 1
    Open your browser and go to https://netlify.com
  • 2
    Click "Sign up" → Sign up with GitHub (or your email)
  • 3
    After signing in, you'll see the Netlify dashboard
  • 4
    Look for the area that says "Sites" and find the drag-and-drop upload area at the bottomIt usually says: "Want to deploy a new site without connecting to Git? Drag and drop your site's output folder here"
  • 5
    Open your File Manager (My Computer/Finder) and find the atu-study-hub folder
  • 6
    Drag the entire atu-study-hub folder and drop it onto the Netlify pageMake sure you're dragging the whole folder, not just one file inside it
  • 7
    ⏳ Wait about 30–60 seconds for the upload to finish
  • 8
    Netlify will give you a link like: https://amazing-falcon-123456.netlify.appClick it to see your live website! Share this link with students.
🎉
Your website is now LIVE! Anyone with the link can visit it. Share it on WhatsApp, Instagram, TikTok — everywhere!
✏️ Give your website a better link
  • 1
    In Netlify, click on your site name
  • 2
    Click "Site configuration" or "Site settings"
  • 3
    Find "Site name" and click "Change site name"
  • 4
    Type: atu-study-hub (or bafiku-study-hub, etc.)
  • 5
    Click Save — your new URL is: https://atu-study-hub.netlify.app
🔄
How to update the website later: When you change any HTML files, just drag the whole atu-study-hub folder onto Netlify again. It replaces the old version automatically.
